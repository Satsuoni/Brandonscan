@BrandSanderson just wondering, do you have any magic underwear ?

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@josephWDye You do realize that what you just asked is somewhat akin to walking up to a Muslim and calling him a towelhead, don’t you?

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Being a fan of your work doesn’t mean that someone shouldn’t call out nonsense when they see or hear it.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele I’m not sure what you’re replying to, exactly.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Sorry, I was responding to your reply to @josephWDye where you compared his question to calling a muslim a towelhead.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Asking, or challenging someone about their beliefs is a good thing to do. Ridiculing them is not.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Where calling a muslim a towelhead is more of a racist/cultural insult, what @josephWDye said addresses a nonsense belief

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Wearing a symbol of my faith is no more a nonsense belief than a Muslim wearing head gear or a Catholic wearing a cross.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele The simile is exactly the same.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson I disagree. A keffiyeh or cross isn’t understood to be magical in the way that the mormon undergarments are. Catholics believing that crackers and wine actually turn into flesh and blood would be similar, and also worthy of ridicule.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele There is nothing magical about them. They are a symbol of faith. Faith may be a protection, true.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Some may regard the garments as having innate protection, but some also believe a cross or Bible does.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Some beliefs are so ridiculous that ridicule is the only appropriate response. And it’s often effective.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Ridicule is an inferior way of dealing with these issues, as it will polarize and demean. Logic and common ground serve better

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson I used to think that as well, but people of ‘faith’ don’t hold their beliefs because of logic and evidence, and so it is next to impossible to demonstrate to them via those avenues that what they believe is false…

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Many do not. There are some who do, however. And I think faith should be founded in experience and evidence.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Then it wouldn’t be faith, it would just be belief. Faith is belief in a proposition in the absense of evidence or in the face of contradictory evidence.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Well, your definition of faith is certainly one. However, I see it more as trusting in something that has been proven to you.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele If my father says, “I will come through for you on such and such” & I trust him because he’s proven in the past, that is faith

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson You’re conflating the term ‘faith’ here. I’m sure you don’t have to be told that a word can have more than one meaning. The meaning here equates to ‘a reasonable expectation based on experience’ That’s not the same as religious faith.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele I am aware of the different definitions. I was trying to define my definition, not say a blanket definition.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele My definition of religious faith, for myself, is exactly what you said. An expectation based on experience.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele As a member of the LDS faith, who has had ample proof given to me of God’s existence—the only logical way I could believe—

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele I find it important to search for the real truths and to understand people who disagree with me, to see if I am wrong.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele I will not go so far as to say that there is never reason for ridicule, however, as I was able to think of a few exceptions.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Ridicule can be effective in demonstrating just how absurd the belief system is from outside…just as all other religions are to the believer. As Heinlein said, “One man’s religion is another man’s belly laugh.”

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Proper ridicule could be appropriate, couched in a form of “Look, can you see how silly this sounds to the outsider?”

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele However, walking up to someone and saying “You are an idiot, and this is stupid” is hardly a good way to begin a dialogue.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Well, I just don’t think that I owe much politeness to people who don’t care in the least for logic and evidence, and are doing their level best to retard science education, repeal social advancements, and who look forward to the destruction of the majority of mankind with anticipation.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele The greatest changes to good people are made through empathy and an attempt to understand.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele We must continue to be polite and strive for common ground. The biggest problem with our discourse these days is—

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele —the justification where people on any side say “I no longer need to be a civil human being to these people because of X.”

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Also, if I vanish, it’s because I am landing and lose internet—not because I’m ignoring you. We could lose it at any moment.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Come on Brandon. I think that the biggest problem with discourse today is that faith gets a free pass. If someone tells you that he believes something on faith, then he thinks that that is unassailable, and I don’t agree.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele If you let your disagreements turn you into hatred and incivility, you become that which you hate, my friend.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele (Sorry to go Yoda on you. That last bit was on the heavy-handed side. )

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Some of the people you speak of are worthy of your contempt. Most are just…well, people.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele The answer to your problems is teaching them to see the other. You cannot do that unless you can do it yourself

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson I think we’re both better than sound-byte platitudes. What I hate is people who enshrine bigotry and ignorance behind a shield of ‘faith,’ while working to destroy everything that the enlightenment has gifted us with.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Faith deserves to be challenged more than it does, and we should not be able to stand behind it as an iron wall.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele I just simply believe that we all need to see one another more as human beings.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Anyhow, good talking to you, even if it is a real pain to do so in these tiny twitter chunks.

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Nice chatting with you too. Again, sorry for the Yoda. You have good arguments. But I think you’re giving in to a simplistic—

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele —view of “Us against them” where “Them” are always trying to destroy everything that is good or virtuous.

jasonmsteele Thu Apr 14
@BrandSanderson Oh, and welcome to Atlanta (where I happen to live)

BrandSanderson Thu Apr 14
@jasonmsteele Thanks! And thank you for reading. I’ll do my best to enjoy Atlanta.
