BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
I’ve been thinking lately of ways to give away digital copies of my books when you buy a hardcover. There are some issues with this. (More.)

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
I don’t know much about the logistics; it may be impossible. If there is a way to make this work, I’d propose it to Tor and Harriet for AMoL

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
Here’s a reddit thread where I mention issues with the process. Weigh in here or in that thread to give me advice. link

Saberj Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson The only way I could think of would be to include one-time use codes with the books. But what’s to stop people from selling?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@Saberj Yeah. The other problem with that is securing the code. Books aren’t wrapped up, so the code could be scratched off/stolen easily.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
My preferred method would be to put a code in a book that, then, you can redeem for free or a small price. But how do we secure it?

nicjohnson Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson You don’t. Your stuff is already being pirated and publishers shouldn’t consider those lost sales. Trust people a la Apple.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@nicjohnson I’m not worried about piracy. However, a digital code that can be used many times seems foolish. A one-use would be stolen.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@nicjohnson One use has to be secured, or the person buying the book is in danger of being ripped off.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@nicjohnson Multi-use means that we’re hosting the book, and paying the bandwidth, for those who want to pirate. Bad idea, I think.

nicjohnson Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson I’ll host it for you. No charge.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@nicjohnson Lol. One other problem is that this needs to be reasonable enough to the publisher’s ears to get them to go along.

nicjohnson Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson My point exactly. Big Pub doesn’t get the new model. They consider pirated copies as lost sales. C Seth Godin for new model

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@nicjohnson The publishers aren’t as ignorant as you think. The investors, however, are another story. (You’re right about them.)

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@nicjohnson Tor has done plenty of giving away free, DRM-free ebooks. They did it with Mistborn, for example.

nicjohnson Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson Ah! To me as an outsider they are one and the same.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@nicjohnson Publishers and editors in sf/f tend to be techies. Notice that Cory D, with Tor’s blessing, releases all of his books for free.

Saberj Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson How is Marvel doing it? They don’t tend to wrap Comics either.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@Saberj I think you order directly from them, and they send you the comic and deliver through their own secure app.

anneburner Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson #baen used to put a CD copy of the book inside the HC versions of @davidweber1 Honor Harrington books.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@anneburner @davidweber1 I asked if we could do that, and the answer was that it was expensive enough it couldn’t become the standard.

doubleewe Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson Maybe like a gift card where it’s only active after purchase?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@doubleewe Yeah, this is probably the best idea. I
don’t know how hard that is to accomplish, though.

Saberj Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson A lot of textbooks used to include a disc in the book for additional material. Discs are a bit harder to steal than codes.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@Saberj Textbooks also have a much larger profit margin than novels. I asked about discs for my last book, and the publisher said no.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@Saberj They said it was just too expensive.

benjymous Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson Old school tech, I know, but how about a coupon you have to fill in with your email address then post back to the publisher?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@benjymous Ha. You know, I never thought about that. The problem is, how do we keep people from stealing them out of the books?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@benjymous People with a nice hardcover don’t want to cut their book apart to get a coupon.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
Here are a couple of problems with what people are suggesting. 1) We don’t want to shrink wrap books, but a code can be stolen very easily.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
Anything involving the retailer verifying a code, or printing one out, requires large-scale involvement of retailers. (more.)

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
That’s not something I can change. They may be working on this already. I want something I could take to Tor, that we could do in house.

Terez27 Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson Or if you’re talking about securing the code in the book…it seems easy enough with textbooks. Peel-off?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@Terez27 People would walk into the store, peel off the sticker, write down the code, then sell it or use it.

brad_lickman Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson how do you stop people from sharing a hardcover copy?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@brad_lickman The physical product can be made to set off an alarm. A code can be copied and carried out. [Assistant Peter’s note: Brandon is all for passing your books around to your friends. Please do!]

andrewyager Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson could codes be single use? That would largely get around the securing problem?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@andrewyager People would walk into the store, write down the code, go home and download the book.

Drantis82 Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson What about one-time scratch codes like what’s used on gift cards?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@Drantis82 Those are usually activated by the retailer. I’d love for us to be able to do that, but it would involve more than I can do.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
Another issue with this is that if I did it, I would need it to work for indy booksellers and not just Amazon/B&N.

brad_lickman Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson can you sell the digital copy at Tor.com, which provides a coupon for the hardcover?

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@brad_lickman This is actually what I proposed to Tor a few years back, and they said they didn’t want to offend the retailers.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@brad_lickman I still like the idea, though.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
I won’t have time to reply to everyone here, but keep sending thoughts. I’ll read and see if I can come up with something to take to Tor.

BenFoxworthy Sat Mar 10
@BrandSanderson How about this: Put the code in the book. Don’t secure it. Each code works 3 times. Hope people don’t abuse it.

BrandSanderson Sat Mar 10
@BenFoxworthy That risks punishing the person who buys the book (but their code has been stolen and used.)
