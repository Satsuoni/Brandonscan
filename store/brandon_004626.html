BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
Question One: “How did you first come to write the Infinity Blade books? #AskBrandon link“

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
It’s a fun story. The creators of the game actually hunted me down, going through Isaac (now one of my assistants) to get dinner with me.

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
They had loved my books, and wanted to do whatever they could to get me working on the project. #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
Having been a gamer since my youth, I was excited about the chance to be involved—and I liked their pitch a lot. #AskBrandon

thefuzzinator Wed Sep 11
@BrandSanderson @iBooks How is Stormlight Archives 2 coming along? #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
WoR is going really well. Working on a big revision right now, about halfway done, and doing a beta read right now. #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
Q: What do you like about writing stories within the world of #InfinityBlade? #AskBrandon link

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
I had never played with the “fantasy world which is really our world in the future” trope, which has a grand tradition in sf/f. #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
Beyond that, the idea of trying to make the logic of a video game make actual sense in a story felt like a cool challenge to me. #AskBrandon

dswint916 Wed Sep 11
#AskBrandon @BrandSanderson Will Marsh continue to shadow the Mistborn series? #Mistborn

In response to @dswint916, yes that character will continue to play a role in the future of Mistborn. (Slight spoilers.) #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
Question: Did your first #InfinityBlade book influence the Infinity Blade II game? #AskBrandon link

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
Yes it did! I was super excited about that. When I came on, the story for the series was fairly unformed.

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
I got to have a lot to do with how the trilogy of games played out storywise, and found it a very engaging process. #AskBrandon

Arithered Wed Sep 11
@BrandSanderson Why do you favor (seemingly) immortal monarch characters? What is your intrigue with them? #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
To @Arithered, I do favor immortal monarchs. This is a function more of how the magic of the cosmere works.

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
But also, I feel that one of the charms of fantasy is dealing with things such as this—individuals who influence the world. @Arithered

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
Question: Where does the new book take the characters as we lead up to Infinity Blade III? #AskBrandon link

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
The original idea was for me to write stories that bridge the three games. #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
That said, book two picks up sometime after the end of the first game. It leads right up to the third game. #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
In fact, the ending of the book is the introductory sequence in the video game, allowing them to blend into one another. #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
Q: How is it different writing Infinity Blade books from writing your other novels? #AskBrandon link

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
The biggest one has to do with working with a video game being three of the five ‘volumes’ in this story. #AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
The games have less space for worldbuilding/characterization, so the bulk of that rests on me—while the action is in the games.#AskBrandon

BrandSanderson Wed Sep 11
In addition, revisions are different since I can change a paragraph easily—but changing a cinematic in-game is really tough. #AskBrandon
